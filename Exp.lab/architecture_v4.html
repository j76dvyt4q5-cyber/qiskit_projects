Quantum Blackjack

A card game where randomness collapses from superposition, and players wield real quantum gate operations — H, X, Z, RY, CX, CZ, CRY — as strategic weapons before each draw.

♠ How to Play
Game Overview
Standard Blackjack rules — with quantum mechanics as the deck
01
You get two cards
Each card is drawn by measuring a quantum circuit in superposition. The result is genuinely random — not a dice roll or RNG seed.
02
Hit, stand, or use gates
On your turn, you can hit for another card, stand to end, or apply quantum gates to bias the next draw before it collapses.
03
Beat 17 without busting
Get closer to 21 than the dealer without going over. The dealer always hits until reaching 17+. Closest to 21 wins.
04
Aces are flexible
Aces count as 11, but automatically soften to 1 if your total would exceed 21. You can hold multiple soft Aces.
05
Gates change the odds
Apply self-gates (to your card register) or table gates (entangling table and card qubits) to tilt probabilities in your favour — or sabotage them.
06
The Ultimate ability
Once per sequence, fire your Ultimate: applies CX gates from both table qubits to matching card qubits — maximum entanglement on the next draw.
I Player Interface
◆
What the player does on each turn
Every turn you choose one action from the prompt. Hit draws immediately. Self and Table queue a gate modifier — it won't take effect until you hit next. Stand locks in your total and hands control to the dealer. Ultimate is a special one-shot entanglement move. Modifiers accumulate and are all cleared after each hit, so you stack them up, then fire.
♠
Action Parser
Reads stdin. Routes to one of five handlers: hit / self / table / ultimate / stand. Falls through to "Invalid action" on anything else.
Rule: You only get one action per turn. Gates don't draw a card — they set up the next draw. You must still hit to resolve them.

Rule: The table register is also in superposition. Entangling it with your card register creates correlated collapse — the most powerful quantum manipulation available.

II Quantum Circuit Engine
ψ
How quantum randomness replaces a deck of cards
In a normal deck, randomness comes from shuffling. Here, randomness comes from quantum superposition. A 4-qubit register is put into a state where all 16 possible cards exist simultaneously — until you measure it. The act of measurement forces the universe to pick one. No shuffling. No seed. Just physics.

Gates applied before measurement tilt the probability amplitudes — some cards become more likely, others less. This is how player strategy maps to quantum mechanics.
⬡
Circuit Factory
Rebuilds a fresh QuantumCircuit every draw: 4 card qubits + 2 table qubits + 4-bit classical register. Hadamard gates on all qubits create equal superposition across all 64 states.
Why rebuild each time? Once you measure a circuit, it collapses. To draw again, you need a fresh superposition — like re-shuffling a new deck every card.
4q card2q tableH⊗6
∿
Gate Applicator
Replays your queued self-gates (H, X, Z, RY) then table→card gates (CRY, CX, CZ) onto the fresh circuit before measurement, in the order they were saved.
Gates bias the draw. X flips a qubit (forces one bit to 1 or 0). RY rotates amplitude smoothly. CX entangles — if the table qubit is |1⟩, it flips your card qubit.

◎
AerSimulator
Transpiles the circuit for Aer and runs it with shots=1. A single shot means one measurement — one collapse — producing exactly one 4-bit classical outcome.
shots=1 is the rule. More shots would give you statistics about many possible cards — but in the game, you draw one card. So you measure once.

↳ Player Gate Reference
Gate Type What it does to your draw Strategic use
H Self Re-applies Hadamard to one card qubit — maintains uniform superposition on that bit. No bias; resets any prior rotation on that qubit. Use to undo a previous gate on a qubit before hitting.
X Self Bit-flip on one card qubit — flips it from |0⟩ to |1⟩ or vice versa. Forces one bit of the card to a definite value, halving the card range. Narrow outcomes toward high-value cards (set bit 3 = 1 → 8–King range).
Z Self Phase-flip on one card qubit — flips the sign of the |1⟩ amplitude. Doesn't change measurement probabilities on its own; useful when combined with other gates. Combine with H for Hadamard-Z-Hadamard = X effect on different basis.
RY(θ) Self Rotates qubit on Y-axis by angle θ. Strength 1/2/3 maps to θ = 0.4/0.8/1.2 rad — tilts probability toward |0⟩ or |1⟩ smoothly. Subtle control. Use strength 3 to strongly bias one bit; strength 1 for gentle nudge.
CRY(θ) Table→Card Conditional RY: only rotates card qubit if the control table qubit is |1⟩. Mode "h" (help) rotates toward |1⟩; mode "s" (sabotage) rotates away. Probabilistic boost — only fires if table qubit collapses to 1 at measurement.
CX Table→Card CNOT: flips a card qubit if the table control qubit is |1⟩. Creates entanglement — card and table outcomes become correlated. Used in the Ultimate. Strongest entanglement move available.
CZ Table→Card Controlled-Z: applies phase flip to card qubit if table qubit is |1⟩. Like CX but in the phase domain — affects interference patterns. Combine with Hadamard on card qubit to produce CX-like effects on superposed states.

III Decode Layer
♠
Turning a bitstring into a playing card
The quantum circuit produces a 4-bit classical string like 1011. This is mapped to a card name — 1011 = Jack. There are 16 possible bitstrings, covering Ace through King plus two Unknown edge cases. Note: Qiskit returns bits in reversed order (qubit 3 first), so the bitstring is flipped before lookup — this ensures that when you target qubit 0 with a gate, it actually controls the lowest bit as expected.
⇄
Bit → Card Mapper
Takes counts dict, extracts the single bitstring key, reverses it with [::-1] (bug fix), then looks it up in a 16-entry dict mapping binary to card names.
Mapping: 0001=Ace, 0010=2 … 1010=10, 1011=Jack, 1100=Queen, 1101=King, 1110=Ace (2nd). 0000 and 1111 return Unknown (value 0).

IV Game State Engine
↻
The game loop and when it ends
The loop runs as long as player_total ≤ 21 and standing == False. Each iteration lets you act once. If you bust (go over 21), standing is set to True and the loop exits immediately — game over. If you stand, the loop exits cleanly and control passes to the dealer. Modifier lists are cleared after every hit — gates are single-use per draw, not permanent.
◈
Setup Phase
set_up() draws card 0, resets the circuit, draws card 1, and prints your opening hand. Both draws use fresh H-initialized circuits with no modifiers.
Rule: Your opening two cards are always drawn from pure superposition — equal probability for all values. Gates only become available after the opening deal.

↻
Game Loop
while (player_total <= 21) and (not standing). Rebuilds circuit with accumulated modifiers on hit, then calls measure_qc_card_cout() to collapse.
Rule: You can apply multiple gates across separate turns before hitting. They stack. But the moment you hit, all queued gates fire at once — then the list resets.

V Dealer Engine
§
How the dealer plays — and why you can't affect it
The dealer follows hard house rules: always hit below 17, always stand at 17+. No choices, no strategy, no gates. Every dealer draw uses a fresh H-initialized circuit with no modifiers — pure 50/50 superposition on every qubit. This mirrors real casino rules, where the dealer's play is deterministic and players have no influence over the dealer's hand after standing.
♥
Dealer Loop
Rebuilds a fresh circuit each iteration. No modifier lists applied. Calls measure_qc_card_cout() directly. Loops until dealer_total ≥ 17.
Rule: Dealer must hit on 16 or below. Dealer must stand on 17 or above. Soft 17 (Ace + 6) also stands. This is the standard Vegas house rule.
hits until 17no modifierspure superposition
♦
Outcome Resolver
Four-way comparison after dealer stands: dealer bust → player wins; dealer total > player → dealer wins; dealer total < player → player wins; equal → tie.
Win conditions: Dealer busting always wins for you regardless of your total. A tie means neither wins. There's no surrender or double-down in this version yet.
